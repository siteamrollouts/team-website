01Introduction

This Cookie Policy explains how Team Rollouts Ltd ("Team", "we", "us") uses cookies and similar technologies on teamrollouts.com and across the Team platform. It should be read alongside our Privacy Policy, which covers how we handle personal data more broadly.

We only set non-essential cookies after you consent. You can change your choice at any time — see managing your preferences.

Quick summary: we use a small number of strictly necessary cookies to run the site, plus optional analytics and marketing cookies that only load if you opt in. Nothing tracks you by default.

02What are cookies

Cookies are small text files that a website places on your device when you visit. They let the site remember things about your visit — like whether you're logged in, which preferences you've set, or whether you've already seen the cookie banner.

This policy also covers similar technologies we may use, including local storage, session storage, pixels, tags, and SDKs. For simplicity we refer to them all as "cookies" throughout.

First-party vs third-party

  • First-party cookies are set by teamrollouts.com directly and readable only by us.
  • Third-party cookies are set by external services we embed (for example, analytics or video providers) and are governed by their own privacy policies.

Session vs persistent

  • Session cookies are deleted automatically when you close your browser.
  • Persistent cookies remain for a set period (shown in retention periods) or until you delete them.

03How we use cookies

We use cookies to:

  • Run the site: serve pages, keep you signed in, and protect against abuse.
  • Remember your choices: including the consent decision you make in our cookie banner.
  • Understand performance: measure which pages and features are useful, in aggregate and never tied to an individual.
  • Measure marketing: understand whether our campaigns are helping the right people discover Team.

We do not use cookies to build advertising profiles about you, sell data to third parties, or track you across unrelated websites.

04Categories we use

The cookie banner on teamrollouts.com groups cookies into three categories. You can accept, reject, or customise them individually.

Strictly necessary

Required for the site to function. These cannot be disabled because the site would break without them — for example, the cookie that stores your consent choice itself, or security cookies that protect against cross-site request forgery. We rely on "legitimate interest" under UK GDPR for these, and they are exempt from consent requirements under PECR.

Analytics (optional — opt in)

Help us understand which pages resonate, what breaks, and where we can improve. Data is aggregated — we do not use it to identify you personally. You can opt in or out at any time with no impact on site functionality.

Marketing (optional — opt in)

Let us measure whether our ads help people discover Team, and show relevant Team content on other platforms. Disable this category and we immediately stop all retargeting and conversion measurement for your session.

06Third-party services

Some pages embed content or tools from third parties. When loaded, these providers may set their own cookies governed by their own policies:

  • Calendly — demo booking (privacy policy). Only loads when you click "Book a demo".
  • HubSpot — forms and CRM tracking (privacy policy). Only loads after you accept the Marketing category.
  • Google Analytics — site performance (privacy policy). Only loads after you accept the Analytics category.
  • Meta — ad measurement (privacy policy). Only loads after you accept the Marketing category.
  • LinkedIn — ad measurement (privacy policy). Only loads after you accept the Marketing category.

We review this list whenever we add a new tool to the site. If a vendor is added that sets cookies, this page is updated before they go live in production.

07Managing your preferences

You're in control of every cookie category except strictly necessary. Change your choice any time:

The same link lives in the footer of every page under "Cookie Preferences". Your choice is stored in a first-party cookie on your device — we do not send your consent record to any third party.

Withdrawing consent

When you withdraw consent for a category, any cookies in that category that were previously set are automatically cleared. You don't need to clear your browser manually.

08Browser-level controls

You can also manage cookies directly in your browser — block them by default, clear them on exit, or delete individual cookies. Instructions for the most common browsers:

  • Google Chrome
  • Safari
  • Firefox
  • Microsoft Edge

Disabling cookies at the browser level may affect the functionality of other websites you use, not just Team. For site-specific control, the cookie preferences modal is usually a better option.

"Do Not Track" and Global Privacy Control

We honour the Global Privacy Control (GPC) signal where it is sent by your browser: if GPC is on, we treat it as an opt-out for Marketing cookies by default. "Do Not Track" (DNT) is a legacy signal that has been widely deprecated and we do not rely on it.

09Retention periods

Retention varies by cookie — specific durations are listed in the tables above. As a general rule:

  • Session cookies expire when you close the browser.
  • Consent record (cc_cookie) is stored for 6 months, after which we re-prompt.
  • Analytics cookies typically expire within 2 years.
  • Marketing cookies typically expire within 13 months, in line with guidance from the UK ICO.

10Legal basis

Team is based in the United Kingdom, so the primary laws that apply are:

  • UK GDPR — the UK's data protection regulation.
  • PECR — the Privacy and Electronic Communications Regulations, which specifically govern cookies and similar technologies in the UK.
  • EU GDPR — for visitors from the EEA.
  • CCPA / CPRA — for visitors from California.

For strictly necessary cookies we rely on legitimate interest and the PECR "strictly necessary" exemption. For all other categories we rely on your consent, which you can withdraw at any time.

11Changes to this policy

We'll update this page whenever we add, remove, or materially change a cookie we use. Minor wording changes (fixing typos, clarifying existing behaviour) won't trigger a reconsent prompt. Material changes — new categories, new vendors, new purposes — will. The "Last updated" date at the top always reflects the most recent change.
